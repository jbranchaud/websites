Research

Being a graduate research assistant and computer science master's student, I do a lot of research. My research interests fall mainly within the area of software engineering, although I find it interesting to bring in research from other areas to suppliment what is done in SE. Currently, I am interested in workspace awareness and team collaboration especially with regard to the ways in which program analysis can be used to inform awareness and collaboration efforts. I am currently working toward my Master's in Computer Science under Dr. Anita Sarma at UNL. Our research efforts are in coordination with others at UCI and CMU through the SCALE Research Group.

Research Projects

  1. Palantir - Workspace Awareness

    January 2010 - present

    Palantir is a workspace awareness tool deployed as an Eclipse plug-in which seeks to inform developers of pertinent activity going on in others' workspaces in between commits. It was developed by Dr. Anita Sarma as part of her doctoral work. During my undergraduate studies, I worked on extracting and modularizing the portion of the plug-in that captures configuration management (subversion) and workspace events. This code has been built into a lightweight Eclipse plug-in that captures and stores these events in a MySQL database. Future work will involve exploiting the information we are capturing to do program analysis and to inform awareness efforts. There is also the possibility of mining this data to learn and report on team collaboration and occurrence of conflicts.
