Lutheran Center Pool League Balls Will Collide

Welcome, this is the official page of the Lutheran Center Pool League. We are an 8-Ball pool league and if you didn't know that, then you may be in trouble. Nevertheless, it is game time and you must bring everything you've got. Sharpen your sticks and polish those balls because it's about to get real and when that blue chalk dust settles there will only be one person (gender inclusivity) standing.

Important We need to come up with league nicknames for all participants! If you do not have a league nickname by the time tournament play starts, then you will be considered ineligible.

Questions? Please ask me in person or drop me an email at jbranchaud@gmail.com. John is also the arbiter of all knowledge, so you can ask him too.

Rules LC League Rules - Know Them

Logistical Rules

  • Important In order to ensure that there is enough time for everyone to play, this rule is of particular importance: You are allotted exactly 45 minutes to play best out of 3 games. This breaks down to 15 minutes per game as a general guideline. If you have exceeded 45 minutes and are still playing at the time that the next match is supposed to start, you must immediately end your game (only collecting the points you have so far, neither player gets the winning bonus).
  • If one of the players is experiencing a time conflict that they are unable to resolve and thus cannot make the game, it is their responsibility to coordinate with the other person to find another time/day to play the match. If the match is not played before the next week of play, the player with the original time conflict must forfeit the game. Six points will be awarded to the other person for that week (3 for each hypothetical game that contributed to their win).
  • If one player wins the first two games, they have won the match (hence, best out of 3). However, if time permits and both players agree, they are welcomed to play a third game. They must agree prior to break whether or not it will be a competitive game (i.e. count towards points and ranking).

Game Play Rules

For the most part, this league will follow the VNEA 8-Ball Rules, so you should know these well and expect to stick to them. There are some areas of ambiguity in the VNEA rules and we have done our best to address any such ambiguity in the bulleted points below. If you come across a situation during league play that doesn't seem to be addressed in the official rules or below, then agree on a fair resolution, be consistent for the rest of the match, and then report the situation to the directors of the league.

  • A player's group of balls (solid/stripe) is determined by successfully making an intentional shot on one of those groups of balls. Any balls made on the break do not consititute an intentional shot (i.e. the table is always still open after the break). If the table is open and an unintentional/illegal shot is made, the table is still open.
  • This is call your pocket pool (as opposed to call your shot) which means that you must identify both the ball that you intend to make and the pocket you intend to make it in. This is not necessary on an obvious shot (i.e. a direct shot), but it is a best practice to always make the distinction. Once the ball and the pocket have been identified, it doesn't matter how it makes it there as long as it goes in (i.e. banks, combos, etc. do not need to be designated). If a non-obvious shot is made without first being identified, it is at the opposing player's disgretion to call for a loss of turn.
  • When a player commits a foul, they lose their turn and their opponent gets the ball in hand anywhere behind the head string and must make a legal shat past the head string (this is the standard 'shoot the ball from inside the kitchen' rule).

Tournament A Single-Elimination Fight to the Death

After the completion of round robin play, we will host a seeded, single-elimination tournament to determine the ultimate pool player in Lutheran Center history. Leave it all on the felt for this one because the gloves are coming off and there's no 'better luck next time.' Finesse, concentration, and a keen eye will serve you well in this tournament, but only those with the most intense blood doping regimen can honestly expect to contend.
